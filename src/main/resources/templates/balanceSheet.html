Fixed assets

Intangible assets

The value of the company's non-physical assets - for example, patents, trademarks, licences, goodwill (taking account of depreciation).

Tangible assets

The value of the company's physical assets used in the business - for example, machinery, fixtures and fittings, office equipment, vehicles, buildings, land (taking account of depreciation).

Investments (fixed assets)

Fixed asset investments are those intended to be held for the medium to long term in order to, for example, generate income - so these would not include investments such as shares in public companies held for resale.

Total fixed assets

Calculation: 'Intangible assets' plus 'tangible assets' plus 'Investments'.

Current assets

Stocks

Sometimes knows as 'inventories' - the value of goods or services bought or made, but not yet sold. For example, raw materials, balance of long-term contracts.

Debtors

Amount owed to the company - for example, debts, payments or income owed but not yet received.

Cash at bank and in hand

Total cash available on demand, either in the bank or in petty cash.

Investments (current assets)

Current asset investments are those held with the intention of selling them in the short term, for example shares in quoted companies intended to be traded.

Total current assets

Calculation: 'Stocks' plus 'debtors' plus 'cash at bank and in hand' plus 'Investments'.
