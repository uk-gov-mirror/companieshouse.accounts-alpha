Tangible assets

Cost

Cost at 1 April 2015

The total cost or valuation of all intangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for.

Additions

The cost of all intangible assets acquired during the period you're filing for.

Disposals

The cost or valuation (at the date of disposal) of all intangible assets that were disposed of (for example, sold) during the period you're filing for.

Revaluations

The valuation figure of all assets revalued during the financial period.

If the revaluation results in a reduction in the value of the asset, then enter a negative figure using a minus sign.

Transfers

The cost or valuation of all intangible assets that have been transferred during the period you're filing for. If the asset is being transferred out, enter a negative figure using a minus sign.

Cost at 31 March 2016

The total cost or valuation of all intangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is calculated by adding additions, revaluations and transfers to the cost or valuation of the assets at the start of the period, then subtracting any disposals.

Depreciation Depreciation is the reduction in value of an asset over its useful lifetime.

Depreciation at 1 April 2015

The total depreciation value of all tangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for.

Charge for year

The total depreciation charged against your profit for the period.

On disposals

The total depreciation value of all tangible assets that were disposed of (for example, sold) during the period you're filing for. This is the depreciation from the date each asset was acquired to the date of its disposal.

Other adjustments

Any other depreciation adjustment on all tangible assets.

Depreciation at 31 March 2016

The total depreciation value of all tangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is calculated by adding tangible assets provided during the period, other adjustments and the depreciation value at the start of the period, then subtracting any disposals.

Net book value Net book value is the original cost of an asset minus any depreciation

Net book value at 31 March 2016

The net book value of all tangible assets at the end of the period you're filing for. This is the total cost or valuation of all tangible assets at the end of the period, minus the total depreciation value of all tangible assets at the end of the previous period.

Net book value at 31 March 2015

The net book value of all tangible assets at the start of the period you're filing for. This is the total cost or valuation of all tangible assets at the start of the period, minus the total depreciation value of all tangible assets at the end of the previous period.
